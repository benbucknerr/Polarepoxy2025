Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an epoxy floor installation take?

The installation time depends on the size and complexity of the project. Typically, a standard residential garage floor can take 2-3 days from start to finish. This includes proper floor preparation, application of multiple coats, and curing time. Commercial or industrial projects may take longer. We'll provide you with a specific timeline during your free consultation.

How much does epoxy flooring cost?

Epoxy flooring costs vary based on several factors including the size of the area, condition of the existing floor, type of epoxy system selected, and decorative options chosen. Our residential epoxy floors typically range from $3-$12 per square foot, while commercial and industrial applications may have different pricing. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your specific project.

How durable are epoxy floors?

Properly installed epoxy floors are extremely durable and can last 15-30 years or more with proper maintenance. Our professional-grade epoxy systems are resistant to impacts, chemicals, stains, and abrasions. They can withstand heavy foot traffic, vehicle traffic, and equipment without showing signs of wear. The longevity also depends on the type of epoxy system chosen and the environment it's installed in.

How do I maintain my epoxy floor?

Epoxy floors are low-maintenance compared to many other flooring options. Regular cleaning involves sweeping or dust mopping to remove dirt and debris, followed by occasional wet mopping with a pH-neutral cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals like ammonia or bleach. Clean spills promptly to prevent staining. For commercial and industrial settings, we can recommend specific maintenance protocols based on your usage patterns.

Are epoxy floors slippery when wet?

Standard epoxy floors can be slippery when wet, but we offer several anti-slip options to increase traction and safety. These include adding anti-slip additives to the final coat, incorporating textured materials, or applying specialized non-slip topcoats. We can customize the level of slip resistance based on your specific needs and the intended use of the space.
